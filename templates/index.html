Preventing Diabetes

  • Data shows that diets, rich in whole grains, help protect against diabetes
    • "Women who averaged 2-3 servings of whole grains a day were 30% less likely to have developed type 2 diabetes than those who rarely ate whole grains."
    • When the researchers combined these results with those of several other large studies, they found that eating an extra two servings of whole grains a day decreased the risk of type 2 diabetes by 21%.

  • The ADA website state:
    • Losing 7-10% of your current weight can cut your chances of developing type 2 diabetes in half.
    • This puts less stress on your insulin-making cells. So trade some of your sit-time for fit-time.
